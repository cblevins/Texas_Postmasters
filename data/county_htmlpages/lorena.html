To Postmasters & Post Offices of McLennan County, Texas

 

LORENA. A settlement on the W. section of
the Missouri Pacific Railway in McLennan county,
90 miles northeast of Austin, and 20 miles south-
west of Waco, the county seat and nearest banking
point. Settled in 1881, it contains 2 churches, a
public school, 2 steam cotton gins, a grist mill
and a corn sheller. Shipments, cotton, corn and
live stock. Population, 150. Tel., Western
Union. Express, Pacific. Mail, daily.  C. F.
Schaefer, postmaster.
 
Aerl Bros., live stock.
Crook, S. J., grocer.
Janes, F. M., cotton gin and coal.
Marky, A., carpenter and cabinetmaker.
Poole, A. J., groceries and hardware.
Schaefer Bros., Gin and Grist Mill, Hay
and General Store.
Snider, E., painter.
Tearkin, Wm., confectioner.
Wood, Dr. W. S., druggist.
 
 
Source: Polk's Texas State Gazeteer & Business Directory,
1884-1885, p. 498.
 
 
 
LORENA. A settlement on the T. section of M., K., & T.
Railway, in McLennan county, 86 miles northeast of Austin,
and 14 miles southwest of Waco, the county seat and nearest
banking point. Settled in 1881, it contains 2 churches, a public
school, a steam cotton gin and grist mill. Population, 250. Tel.,
Western Union. Express, Pacific. J. L. Walker, postmaster.
 
Aerl Bros., live stock.
